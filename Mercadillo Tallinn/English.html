Tallinn Christmas Market

Description

In keeping with tradition, Tallinn's Christmas market will open its stalls this year to give us the pleasure of enjoying a fairytale Christmas. On Raekoja Plats, the Town Hall Square, stands a grandiose fir tree, the symbol of Christmas, and our city is proud to be the first place in the world to erect a Christmas tree: in 1441, no less!

In recent years, the market as a whole with the beautiful medieval buildings of Raekoja Plats, has become a symbol of Christmas and tourism, not only in Estonia, but throughout Europe, being rated as one of the best Christmas markets on the continent and the world, receiving each year almost a million visitors from all over the world. Children will have guaranteed fun thanks to Santa Claus, who in his wooden house gives candy to children who best recite verses (singing and reciting verses or poems is a tradition of our country shared with our Latvian and Lithuanian Baltic neighbors). At the various booths you can buy everything from high quality handicrafts to winter accessories such as gloves or scarves. And when you've had enough of browsing the stalls and find your stomach growling, you can satisfy your appetite with some ginger candy or taste delicious Estonian grilled dishes. You can also try mulled wine, a typical drink of the Baltic countries, Scandinavia and Russia. Or the international hot chocolate, which always feels great. The Christmas spirit is present, and reinforced by a special cultural program. On weekends, different music groups from Estonia and abroad will play on the flea market stage, and every Sunday during the Advent period a candle will be lit, at the same time as the pastors of the Estonian and Russian Lutheran churches and the mayor of the city will give welcoming speeches to the people on the square.And 2019 will be welcomed with an even more special program!
Tallinn Christmas Market welcomes everyone with open arms. Merry Christmas.


Calendar

The flea market is open from November 16 to January 7 every day from 10:00 to 20:00. Hot drinks (mulled wine) can be enjoyed from Sunday to Thursday until 22:00, and on Fridays and Saturdays until 23:00. Special Christmas program: Fridays from 17:00 to 19:00, Saturdays and Sundays from 12:00 to 14:00. From December 2 Santa Claus welcomes children from Monday to Sunday from 12:00 to 19:00.

Place

Raekoja Plats is in the heart of the historic centre of Tallinn. For those tourists staying outside the centre or living in the periphery, the city bus and tram stops are only a 5-minute walk away. Tram line 4 directly connects Raekoja Plats to the airport.

Nearest bus stop: Viru (lines 21, 21B, 40, 41, 48, 73)
